Site Author
 Dani Anguiano and Lois Beckett


Abstract
 This article reports the outcome and victory for Hollywood writers of the recent writer's strike of this year. It details one of the main reasons for the strike, which was possibilty of studios using AI for writing. The terms agreed to do not outlaw the use of AI, but there are gaurdrails in place so that this technology stays in the control of the workers, rather than being used by studios to replace them.

 Agreement on Key AI Terms Leads to Writer's Strike Resolution: What Employers Need to Know
Site Author
 Landon R. Schwob and Todd A. Lyon
Abstract
 This article details the key points of the new agreement for Hollywood writers when it comes to AI. The four main points include: AI cannot be used to write or rewrite original material, and its content will not be considered over actual writer's work, writer's can use AI only if they agree to use it, the company must disclose to writer's if any material given to them is AI generated, and AI has been prohibited to being trained using writer's material.
